Types of car racing...

  • Exposed wheel racing (formula and indie) Single seat cars are usually the most extreme and fastest sports cars in the racing world. They usually have an open wheel (not covered by the body of the car) and a chassis made of lightweight, rigid materials.
  • Rally
  • NASCAR NASCAR racing is held with mass-produced or similar serial cars at a variety of levels of improvement. The races in the United States are held in several series based on the rules of the NASCAR supervisory body. It is the most popular motorsport in the United States and its races have been held since the 1950s. The race tracks in the NASCAR series are paved and circular. A distinctive feature is the use of oval tracks - 34 of the 36 races take place on tracks of this type - which competitors orbit hundreds of times.
    • Postscript-viewer-blue.svg expanded entry – World Rally Championship The Citroën C4 WRC Rally car driven by Sebastian Loeb in 2007 Rally racing is races held on public roads on non-circular routes. The route of the race usually includes paved roads (temporarily closed), dirt paths and even ice or snow. The participating cars may be based on serial cars but not necessarily. The World Rally Championship (WRC) is an annual series held worldwide and featuring a championship for drivers and manufacturers. The cars are suitable for driving in the different terrains and equipped with all-wheel drive.
